MAS MindForge AI Risk Management Framework

The Monetary Authority of Singapore's AI Risk Management Toolkit for financial institutions — covering traditional AI, generative AI, and emerging agentic AI technologies. Applicable to all MAS-regulated entities.

What is MindForge?

Project MindForge is the MAS's industry-led initiative to develop a comprehensive AI Risk Management Toolkit for the Singapore financial sector. Launched in 2023, it concluded Phase 2 in March 2026 with the publication of the full toolkit developed by a consortium of 24 leading financial institutions.

The toolkit spans traditional AI, generative AI, and agentic AI — and is accompanied by the MAS AI Risk Management Guidelines issued via consultation in November 2025, with a 12-month transition period for compliance.

Scope of Application

The FEAT Principles
Foundation of MAS responsible AI — Fairness, Ethics, Accountability, Transparency
F
Fairness

AI systems must not produce unfairly biased outcomes. Define fairness metrics, monitor for demographic disparities, and document bias assessments for all customer-facing AI.

→ Veritas Framework for measurement
E
Ethics

AI systems must serve legitimate purposes without creating disproportionate harm to customers or communities. Annual ethics review of societal impact required.

→ Annual AI impact assessment
A
Accountability

Clear accountability from the boardroom to the model development team. Designated governance functions, escalation procedures, and three lines of defence with AI-specific responsibilities.

→ 3LoD model for AI risk
T
Transparency

AI decisions must be explainable to customers, regulators, and internal audit. Model documentation, audit trails, and explainability outputs required for high-materiality systems.

→ AI Verify toolkit for testing
Four Framework Sections
17 Considerations across Scope, Risk Management, Lifecycle, and Enablers
Section 1
Scope & AI Oversight
Establish AI governance structures and clarify oversight roles and responsibilities across the institution — from the board to individual model owners.
  • Establishing clear AI governance operating models with board accountability
  • Defining AI-related policies, procedures, and standards
  • Integrating AI risks into the enterprise risk framework
  • Enhancing third-party AI risk management practices
Section 2
AI Risk Management
Identify AI usage across the organization, assess risk materiality using the Impact × Complexity × Reliance model, and maintain a comprehensive AI inventory.
  • Implementing use case-level risk management with proportionate controls
  • Maintaining AI inventory capabilities (all AI — internal, third-party, open-source)
  • Assessing use case context and design compatibility
  • Evaluating data usage compatibility
  • Adopting appropriate data management practices
  • Evaluating third-party AI product risks
Section 3
AI Lifecycle Management
Implement controls covering the entire AI lifecycle — from design and development through deployment, ongoing monitoring, change management, and retirement.
  • Building appropriate guardrails and performance metrics
  • Conducting pre-deployment testing and review
  • Developing monitoring and contingency plans
  • Conducting ongoing monitoring in production
  • Managing changes through effective change management
Section 4
Enablers
Develop the organizational capabilities, infrastructure, and resources needed for sustainable, responsible AI use and ongoing risk management at scale.
  • Building employee AI governance skills and culture across all three lines of defence
  • Supporting AI deployment fitness (infrastructure, tooling, resourcing)
Risk Materiality Assessment
Three dimensions determine the level of governance control required for each AI system
I
Impact

Potential consequences to the institution, customers, and stakeholders — covering financial, operational, regulatory, and reputational effects if the AI system fails or produces biased outcomes.

C
Complexity

Derived from the technology's novelty, data characteristics (volume, variety, sensitivity), evolving AI capabilities, and the degree to which the system's behavior can be predicted and explained.

R
Reliance

The degree of autonomy granted to the AI system — and inversely, the availability of meaningful human oversight. High reliance on AI output without human review requires stronger controls.

GenAI & Agentic AI Requirements
MindForge specific guidance for emerging AI technologies

Generative AI Controls

  • Hallucination detection and human review workflows for AI-generated outputs
  • Prompt injection prevention — adversarial input testing before deployment
  • Training data leakage safeguards — ensure PII and confidential data is not surfaced
  • Output filtering and content guardrails appropriate to the use case context
  • FEAT Transparency — disclosure to customers when interacting with GenAI
  • Human-in-the-loop mandatory for high-impact or regulated GenAI decisions

Agentic AI Requirements

  • Define explicit action boundaries — what the agent can and cannot do autonomously
  • Safe action limits aligned with business objectives and risk appetite
  • Human escalation protocols with clear trigger conditions
  • Real-time intervention capability — kill-switch and circuit breaker mechanisms
  • Audit trail for every autonomous action with rationale and alternatives considered
  • Multi-agent systems: individual materiality assessment for each agent in the system
MindForge Compliance by Maturity Stage
How MindForge obligations scale with your AI adoption stage
Stage 1
Exploring
MindForge Baseline: Governance Foundations

Conduct gap assessment against all 17 Considerations. Establish board-level AI risk appetite statement. Create initial AI inventory (even a spreadsheet). Begin FEAT principles awareness training. Assign AI risk owner in 2nd line.

Stage 2
Experimenting
MindForge Core: Policies, Inventory, Lifecycle Controls

Implement AI governance policies (Consideration 2). Formalize AI inventory with materiality classifications (Consideration 6). Apply materiality assessment to all pilots. Run FEAT-aligned bias testing. Establish pre-deployment testing gates (Consideration 12). Begin 3LoD integration.

Stage 3
Scaling
MindForge Operationalized: All 17 Considerations Active

All 17 Considerations mapped to documented controls. Automated FEAT fairness monitoring via Veritas. AI Verify toolkit integrated into CI/CD pipeline. GenAI-specific controls deployed. Third-party AI risk program active (Considerations 4 & 10). Board-level AI risk reporting quarterly.

Stage 4
Transforming
MindForge Leadership: Agentic AI, Standards Shaping

Full agentic AI governance framework deployed. Participating in MAS BuildFin.ai workgroup shaping next-generation MindForge standards. Annual independent MindForge compliance review. Published AI transparency report. MindForge-certified AI platform offered to peers.

MAS Supporting Tools & Initiatives
Open-source frameworks published by MAS to help institutions implement MindForge

Veritas Initiative

Provides FEAT assessment methodology, fairness metrics libraries, and templates for operationalizing the Fairness principle in credit, marketing, and customer service AI. Free toolkit published by MAS.

→ FEAT Fairness measurement

AI Verify Toolkit

Open-source testing framework for assessing AI models on fairness, transparency, and robustness. Generates standardized test reports for regulatory submission. Developed by IMDA and MAS.

→ MindForge Consideration 12

MindForge Operationalisation Handbook

Detailed practical guidance on implementing each of the 17 Considerations, with case studies from 24 consortium institutions. Includes templates, tools, and worked examples.

→ mas.gov.sg/project-mindforge

BuildFin.ai Workgroup

MAS-convened workgroup developing next-generation AI risk management standards, with initial focus on agentic AI governance. Open to MindForge consortium members and invited industry experts.

→ Stage 4 engagement

MAS Regulatory Sandbox

Fast-track regulatory environment for testing novel AI financial products and services under MAS supervision. Allows pre-clearance of innovative AI approaches with reduced regulatory risk.

→ Agentic AI / novel use cases

FEAT Self-Assessment Guide

MAS-published guide helping institutions self-assess their AI systems against each FEAT principle. Structured questionnaire format with scoring guidance and remediation recommendations.

→ FEAT Principles implementation
