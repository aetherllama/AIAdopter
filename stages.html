Maturity Framework

The Four AI Maturity Stages

A finance-sector-specific framework measuring AI adoption across five dimensions. Score 0–100 across Strategy, Data, Talent, Use Cases, and Risk & Compliance.

Five Dimensions — 20 Points Each

  • ◈ Strategy & Governance — AI strategy, exec sponsorship, ethics
  • ▦ Data & Infrastructure — Cloud, pipelines, data quality
  • ◎ AI Talent & Culture — Team, literacy, experimentation culture
  • ▲ Use Cases in Production — Live deployments, MLOps, ROI
  • ⊕ Risk, Compliance & IP — SR 11-7, EU AI Act, model monitoring

Stage Classification Thresholds

  • Stage 1 — Exploring: Score 0–39
  • Stage 2 — Experimenting: Score 40–59
  • Stage 3 — Scaling: Score 60–79
  • Stage 4 — Transforming: Score 80–100
  • Each dimension scored 1–5 per question, normalized to 0–20
  • 24 questions across all 5 dimensions
Stage 1
Exploring
Score 0–39 · Early Awareness
Stage 1

Characteristics

  • No formal AI strategy or board-level AI commitment
  • Isolated experiments driven by individuals, not programs
  • Data governance is informal; siloed data lakes
  • No dedicated AI team — borrowed resources from IT
  • AI knowledge concentrated in 1–2 people
  • No production AI deployments; only PoCs or vendor demos

Typical AI Use Cases

  • Vendor-provided credit scoring (black box)
  • Basic RPA / process automation
  • Chatbot from an off-the-shelf vendor
  • Excel-based predictive analytics

Institution Archetype

  • Community banks & credit unions
  • Regional insurers building first digital capabilities
  • Wealth managers just beginning digital transformation
Stage 2
Experimenting
Score 40–59 · Active Pilots
Stage 2

Characteristics

  • AI strategy document exists but not yet board-ratified
  • Small AI team forming (3–8 data scientists/ML engineers)
  • 1–2 use cases in production; remaining are pilots
  • Basic cloud infrastructure in place for model training
  • Model risk framework adopted but lightly enforced
  • Business lines aware of AI but not deeply engaged

Typical AI Use Cases

  • Transaction fraud detection (in-house ML)
  • Credit underwriting model (first generation)
  • Customer churn prediction
  • Document extraction for loan processing
  • KYC / CDD automation

Institution Archetype

  • Mid-size regional banks ($10B–$100B assets)
  • Super-regional insurers with emerging data science teams
  • Asset managers beginning systematic/quantitative strategies
Stage 3
Scaling
Score 60–79 · Enterprise AI
Stage 3

Characteristics

  • Board-approved AI strategy tied to business objectives
  • AI team of 20–60 with MLOps, product, and risk specialists
  • AI deployed across 3+ business lines in production
  • MLOps platform enables rapid, reliable model deployment
  • Automated model monitoring and drift detection
  • Structured AI literacy programs for business users
  • SR 11-7 fully implemented with independent validation

Typical AI Use Cases

  • Next Best Action (NBA) personalization engine
  • Real-time payments fraud (sub-100ms ML)
  • CECL / loan loss forecasting with ML
  • Algorithmic trading signal generation
  • AI-assisted compliance monitoring
  • Network analytics for fraud rings

Institution Archetype

  • Large regional banks ($100B+ assets)
  • Top-25 life insurers with AI Centers of Excellence
  • Systematic hedge funds and quantitative asset managers
Stage 4
Transforming
Score 80–100 · AI-First Institution
Stage 4

Characteristics

  • AI-first culture: every product roadmap includes AI by default
  • Chief AI Officer with P&L accountability
  • 100+ production AI models across all business lines
  • Autonomous AI decisioning in selected use cases (STP)
  • Proprietary fine-tuned LLMs on institutional data
  • AI patent portfolio; publishing AI research externally
  • Shaping industry standards and regulatory guidance

Typical AI Use Cases

  • Autonomous loan underwriting (full STP)
  • Sovereign LLM for internal research and compliance
  • Multi-agent AI orchestration across business lines
  • AI-native treasury and ALM management
  • AI platform monetization (BaaS APIs for peers)
  • Correspondent banking network risk analytics

Institution Archetype

  • Global systemically important banks (G-SIBs)
  • Leading global insurers with in-house AI labs
  • Top quant funds and AI-native neobanks
