Common Gaps at Stages 1–2

  • No executive sponsor or Chief AI Officer accountable for outcomes
  • Data quality issues blocking first AI pilots from launching
  • Model risk management framework missing or informal
  • AI team headcount too small to sustain concurrent projects
  • Business units not involved in defining AI use cases
  • No clear AI strategy document approved at board level

What Stage 3–4 Institutions Do Differently

  • MLOps platform enables 10× faster model deployment cycles
  • AI embedded across 5+ business lines with dedicated line AI leads
  • Automated model monitoring catches drift before regulators do
  • AI literacy training extends to front-line and relationship managers
  • Governance frameworks scale — 50+ models managed systematically
  • Proprietary data assets and fine-tuned models create durable AI moats

Stage 2
Experimenting

Active pilots in one or two business lines. Small AI team forming. Basic governance in place but scaling is difficult.

Stage 3
Scaling
Score 60–79

AI deployed across multiple business lines with MLOps infrastructure, systematic governance, and measurable ROI.

Stage 4
Transforming
Score 80–100

AI-first institution with autonomous decisioning, proprietary models, and industry leadership. AI drives competitive advantage.
